Thailand

Som Tum

25 MINS SERVES 4

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 2 tbsp palm or dark brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp dried shrimp, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 lb Chinese long beans or green beans
  • 1 (1.5-lb) unpeeled green papaya
  • 10 large cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 fresh red Thai chile with seeds, sliced
  • 2 tbsp salted peanuts, coarsely chopped

Instructions

01

Make the Dressing: Whisk the lime juice, sugar, fish sauce, dried shrimp, and garlic in a medium bowl until the sugar dissolves. Set aside.

02

Prep the Beans: Cook beans in a medium saucepan of boiling salted water until crisp-tender (about 5 minutes). Rinse under cold water to stop the cooking, then cut into 2-inch pieces.

03

Shred the Papaya: Using a julienne peeler or box grater, peel enough of the green papaya to measure 6 cups. Place in a large mixing bowl.

04

Toss & Serve: Add the tomatoes, cilantro, green onions, chile, and blanched green beans to the papaya. Pour the dressing over the top and toss well. Sprinkle with peanuts and serve immediately.

Ingredient Tip: Palm sugar, fish sauce, dried shrimp, Chinese long beans, green papaya, and Thai chiles can all easily be found at your local Asian market.
