Fish and Chips

45 MINS PREP SERVES 6

Ingredients

  • 6 large russet potatoes
  • Vegetable oil, for deep-frying
  • 6 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt, plus more for seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon freshly ground black pepper, plus more for seasoning
  • 3 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 3 (12-ounce) cans soda water
  • 1 to 2 cups rice flour
  • 6 cod fillets, sliced diagonally into 1/2-inch-wide strips

Instructions

01

Prep the Potatoes: Peel the potatoes and cut them into strips that are 1/4 inch thick and 3 inches long. In a deep-sided pot, heat 3 inches of oil to 325°F. Fry the potatoes until they have softened and become slightly browned but not crisp, 2 to 3 minutes. Remove the chips with a large slotted spoon and set them aside on paper towels.

02

Mix the Batter: Increase heat until the oil reaches a temperature of 375°F. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, baking powder, salt, pepper, and eggs. Pour in the soda water and whisk until the batter is smooth.

03

Dredge the Fish: Spread the rice flour on a separate plate. Dredge the fish pieces in it, then dip them into the batter, letting the excess drip off.

04

The Final Fry: Put the chips in the bottom of a fryer basket (or use a slotted spoon) and carefully submerge them in the hot oil. Carefully add the fish to the bubbling oil. Fry until crispy and brown, 4 to 5 minutes. Remove and drain on paper towels; season lightly with salt and pepper. Serve with Aioli and Mignonette Dipping Sauce.
